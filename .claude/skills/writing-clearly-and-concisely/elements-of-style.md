# Elements of Style — Compact

Apply these when editing prose. LLM-authored drafts rarely have grammar errors (possessives, comma splices, dangling modifiers) — skip straight to composition unless the text is clearly human-drafted and rough.

## Composition (the rules that matter most)

**Active voice.** Name the actor; put it as the subject. Two disguises to watch for: passive perfect (*have been addressed* → *the fixes address them*) and actor-dropped simple passive (*the document is divided into chunks* → *the system divides it*). *is improving* → *has improved* (say it happened, don't narrate progress).

**Positive form.** Say what is, not what isn't.
- *did not remember* → *forgot*
- *is not always perfect* → name the failure mode (*loses accuracy when context spans sections*)

**Specific and concrete.** Name the thing, the number, the actor. *decreased significantly* → *dropped 12%*. *a vehicle* → *a Ford pickup*.

**Omit needless words.** Every word earns its place. Cut on sight:
- *the fact that, due to the fact that, owing to the fact that* → delete, or *because*
- *in order to* → *to*; *is/are able to* → *can*
- *there is/there are ... that/which* → rewrite with the real subject as subject
- *it is worth noting that, it should be mentioned that* → delete, just say the thing
- *in many cases* → delete, or *often* / *usually*
- *at this point in time, currently* → *now*
- Stock closers that restate nothing new (*the overall state has improved*, *more work remains*, *requires careful handling*) → cut, or state the one real next step
- *very, really, quite, rather* → delete, or use one stronger word
- *utilize* → *use*; *in terms of, with regard to* → *about*, or rephrase

**Don't hedge facts with weak modals.** *can help privacy* → *strengthens privacy*. *may include indexing* → *includes indexing* (unless genuinely optional — then say what determines it). *should come first* → *comes first*. Reserve *can/may/should* for real possibility, not a tic.

**Collapse "so that X can Y."** *so the assistant can receive relevant background* → *giving the assistant relevant background*.

**End on the strong word.** Put the emphatic phrase last, not buried mid-sentence.

**Parallel structure.** Match grammatical form across a series: *swimming, biking, and to run* → *swimming, biking, and running*.

**Keep related words together.** Put modifiers next to what they modify.

**One topic per paragraph, topic sentence first.** Split paragraphs that drift; don't bury the point.

## Grammar (rarely needed on LLM output — check only for human-drafted or rough text)
Possessive +'s even after s · comma before "and/or" in a 3+ list · pair parenthetical commas or drop both · don't splice independent clauses with just a comma · an opening participial phrase must modify the real subject · one tense per summary.

Cut ruthlessly; don't cut meaning. When in doubt, prefer the version with fewer words and no hedges that says the same thing.
